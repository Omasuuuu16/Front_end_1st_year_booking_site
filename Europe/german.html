Welcome to Germany's Highlights

Neuschwanstein Castle

Neuschwanstein Castle, located in Bavaria, Germany, is a 19th-century masterpiece built by King Ludwig II. Known for its romantic design and stunning Alpine surroundings, it inspired iconic fairy tale castles like Disney's. Today, it’s a major tourist attraction visited by millions worldwide. more info

Brandenburger Tor

Brandenburger Tor (Brandenburg Gate) is a renowned national symbol in Berlin, Germany, built in 1791 in the neoclassical style. Located in Pariser Platz, it features the Quadriga statue of a chariot drawn by four horses, symbolizing victory. During the Cold War, it stood as a symbol of division near the Berlin Wall, and after the wall's fall in 1989, it became a symbol of German unity. Today, it is one of Berlin's top tourist attractions and a site for national celebrations. more info

Kölner Dom

Kölner Dom (Cologne Cathedral) is a magnificent Gothic cathedral in Cologne, Germany, and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Built over 600 years, it was completed in 1880 and is famous for its twin spires, standing at 157 meters. The cathedral houses the Shrine of the Three Kings and is one of Germany's most visited landmarks, symbolizing faith and architectural mastery. more info
