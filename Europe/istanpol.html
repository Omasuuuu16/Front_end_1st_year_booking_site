Topkapi Palace

Topkapi Palace is a historic symbol of the Ottoman Empire. Built in the 15th century, it served as the sultans' residence for centuries. Now a museum, it showcases treasures like imperial jewels and religious relics, offering a glimpse into Ottoman history.more info

Princes' Islands

The Princes' Islands are a group of serene islands near Istanbul, offering a peaceful escape from the city's hustle. Known for their car-free streets and charming views, the islands are perfect for relaxing walks or cycling. Visitors can enjoy stunning landscapes, historic mansions, and delicious seafood, making them a must-visit destination. more info

Blue Mosque

The Blue Mosque, also known as the Sultan Ahmed Mosque, is one of Istanbul's most iconic landmarks. Built in the 17th century, it is famous for its stunning blue tiles, grand domes, and six minarets. The mosque remains a place of worship and a symbol of Ottoman architectural brilliance. more info
