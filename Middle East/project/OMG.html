Middle East

The Middle East is a culturally rich and strategically vital region that bridges Asia, Africa, and Europe. Known for its deep historical roots, religious significance, and vast reserves of oil and gas, the Middle East has played a central role in global affairs for centuries. Its diverse landscapes, from desert dunes to fertile river valleys, are home to a wide range of peoples, traditions, and political systems.

Egypt

Egypt, located in the northeastern corner of Africa, is a country rich in history, culture, and geographic significance. Known as the cradle of one of the world's earliest civilizations, Ancient Egypt, it is home to iconic landmarks and the Nile River—the longest river in the world.

Algeria, the largest country in Africa, stretches from the Mediterranean coast to the Sahara Desert. It has a rich history shaped by Berber, Arab, and French influences, and today it stands as a key player in North African politics and energy production.

Saudi Arabia

Saudi Arabia, located on the Arabian Peninsula, is known for its vast deserts, Islamic heritage, and economic influence as the world's largest oil exporter. Its capital, Riyadh, is a hub of modernization, while cities like Mecca and Medina are sacred to millions of Muslims worldwide.
